# Copy Research Notes — Harry Dry + Kallaway-Inspired Hook System

## What was accessible

- Harry Dry's Marketing Examples pages were reachable from this runtime.
- Public search for “Kallaway” was noisy/ambiguous and primarily returned an unrelated UK communications agency or irrelevant search results. Use the user's requested name as a style direction, but do not claim verified Kallaway source specifics from this run.

## Harry Dry / Marketing Examples principles to encode

Harry Dry's style is useful because it is:

1. Short and concrete.
2. Example-led, not abstract.
3. Built on contrast: before/after, expected/actual, obvious/hidden.
4. High signal: no throat clearing.
5. Easy to repeat verbally.
6. Specific enough to make the reader think “that's me.”
7. Uses curiosity gaps without sounding clickbait.

## Hook patterns for Cherry

Avoid generic hooks:

- “Social anxiety is hard.”
- “You need confidence.”
- “Stop avoiding people.”

Use high-specificity hooks:

- “The worst part is when they see you seeing them.”
- “You didn't ignore them. You panicked quietly.”
- “You opened the text because not opening it felt worse.”
- “Cancelling plans has a half-life.”
- “The reply was four words. Somehow it became a project.”

## Variable slide length rule

8 slides is often too long for Cherry. Use varying lengths:

- 4 slides: sharp confession / single idea.
- 5 slides: hook → twist → consequence → tiny reframe → soft CTA.
- 6 slides: best default for Cherry.
- 7 slides: only when the idea needs a payoff.

Do not force every script to 8 slides.

## Cherry pacing target

Slide rhythm:

1. specific/private hook,
2. correction/twist,
3. emotional cost,
4. simple reframe,
5. tiny rep / Cherry soft mention.

The product should feel like the natural answer, not the main character.
