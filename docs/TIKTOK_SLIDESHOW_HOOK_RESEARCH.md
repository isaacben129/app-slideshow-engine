## Strongest verified principles

### 1. The first slide is the ad

TikTok says to prioritize the hook early, introduce the content proposition quickly, and use suspense, surprise, or emotion. Carousel-specific sources consistently say the first slide has roughly 1.5–3 seconds to earn the swipe.

For Cherry: slide 1 must be understandable instantly without context.

Bad:

- social anxiety is hard
- you need confidence
- try this social anxiety app

Better:

- the worst part is when they see you seeing them
- the reply was four words
- cancelling plans has a half-life

### 2. Recognition beats explanation

For mental-health/self-improvement slideshows, the hook should create self-recognition before teaching or selling.

Useful formula:

> name the private moment the viewer has never seen named

Examples:

- you rehearse the sentence, then avoid the person anyway
- the text is not scary until it says “seen”
- you did not ignore them. you panicked quietly

### 3. Specificity beats intensity

Harry Dry-style copy and TikTok hook research both reward specificity. Specific situations create more trust than broad emotional claims.

Bad:

- anxiety ruins your life

Better:

- you changed aisles to avoid saying hi

### 4. Tension creates the swipe

A hook should open a loop. It should imply slide 2 will resolve or deepen something.

Patterns:

- contradiction: “you wanted to go. then you cancelled.”
- consequence: “the relief lasted twelve minutes.”
- hidden cost: “your brain still counted it.”
- social friction: “now not saying hi feels like a decision.”

### 5. Do not lead with the app

TikTok and ad-hook sources warn against branded intros. The product comes after attention is earned.

For Cherry: app mention should usually land on the final slide or caption, not slide 1.

### 6. Text hooks should be short

Multiple sources recommend short hooks. Metadata Reactor explicitly claims six words maximum for video text hooks, but carousel slides can tolerate slightly more if the line is emotionally precise. For Cherry:

- ideal slide 1: 5–11 words
- avoid more than two lines on slide 1
- line breaks should add rhythm, not force reading

### 7. Carousels should be concise

UseVisuals says practical TikTok carousel length is often 3–10 slides. For Cherry, 8 slides felt too long. Current rule:

- 4 slides: sharp confession/single idea
- 5 slides: best default
- 6 slides: deeper emotional loop
- 7 slides: only if payoff needs setup
- avoid forced 8-slide scripts

## Hook categories to use for Cherry

### A. Private Moment Hook

Formula:

> the exact small thing they do + the emotional contradiction

Examples:

- you saw them first, so now it counts
- the reply was four words
- you opened the text because not opening it felt worse
- you walked slower so you would not arrive together

### B. Consequence Hook

Formula:

> tiny avoidance + delayed cost

Examples:

- cancelling plans has a half-life
- the relief lasted twelve minutes
- the silence got bigger overnight
- you skipped the call and still felt watched

### C. Correction Hook

Formula:

> it was not [moral judgment], it was [real mechanism]

Examples:

- you were not rude. you froze.
- you did not ignore them. you panicked quietly.
- you are not lazy. the ask got too loud.
- it was not nothing. your brain logged it.

### D. Counterintuitive Hook

Formula:

> common advice is wrong/backwards for this person

Examples:

- confidence is the wrong starting point
- “just say hi” skips the hard part
- the goal is not becoming outgoing
- exposure fails when the first rep is too big

### E. Question Hook

Formula:

> question that only the target avatar feels in their body

Examples:

- why does “hey” feel like a cliff?
- why is replying harder after “seen”?
- why does relief turn into guilt so fast?

Use questions sparingly; statements feel stronger for Cherry.

### F. Proof/Experiment Hook

Works better after Cherry has analytics or user stories.

Examples:

- one tiny rep beat a week of planning
- this is what happened after 7 small reps

Do not fake proof.

## Hooks to avoid

Avoid generic TikTok bait:

- stop scrolling if...
- you need to hear this
- social anxiety tips that changed my life
- unpopular opinion: social anxiety...
- nobody talks about social anxiety
- this app cured my anxiety

Avoid vague therapy language:

- heal your nervous system
- unlock your confidence
- overcome fear forever

Avoid claims Cherry cannot prove:

- cure
- guaranteed confidence
- clinically proven unless verified

## Cherry hook scorecard

Score each candidate 0–2 on each dimension:

1. Private specificity — names an exact moment.
2. Recognition — target user thinks “that is me.”
3. Tension — creates an open loop for slide 2.
4. Brevity — readable in ~2 seconds.
5. Non-generic — avoids stock TikTok phrasing.
6. Non-salesy — does not lead with Cherry/app/product.
7. Visual compatibility — works on one symbolic pixel-art background.

Minimum score for scheduling: 10/14.
